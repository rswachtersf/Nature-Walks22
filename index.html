Our Nature Outings

2021-22 SFIS 5th Grade

The Project


The My Nature Outing Website Unit combines three fields of study: Environmental Justice, Data Science, and Computer Science. In this unit, students are introduced to Environmental Justice as they participate in a nature walk around their school community. They learn that humans have an impact on their environment and that when decisions are made concerning environmental regulations that everyone, regardless of race, income, or location should have access to a healthy environment. Students collect, analyze, and report on qualitative data throughout the unit. While on their nature walk, students collect images of nature to share what they see on their walk. Additionally, collect data through their senses. They write down what they see, hear, feel, and smell on their walk. They also interview classmates, family members, or professionals about their experiences with nature or their opinions on environmental issues. They report all of their findings, images, and interviews on their websites. Students use Computer Science throughout this unit as well. They learn about file management as they transfer image files from an iPod to their Google Drive to a folder on their desktop. They remix an index.HTML file template to begin learning the structure of HTML code. They add their images, text, and sound files to the HTML code as they build their website incrementally and iteratively. In the end, every student has a publishable website telling about their experience in nature.

Descriptive Writing

Students start by collecting 5 to 20 words (nouns, verbs, and adjectives) to describe what they experienced on their nature walk. They can look at their pictures, collect names of trees, plants, animals, insects, and other things they saw on their nature walk. They are encouraged to think about their other senses as well. They should include words to describe what they heard, smelled, or maybe felt.

Once they have created their word bank, they have their choice of writing a descriptive paragraph about their time in nature or a poem in whatever form they choose. They should try to include as many of the words from their word bank in their writing.


Audio Assets


Students interview one another or a family member about their experiences with nature.


Students are introduced to the term Environmental Justice.

“Environmental Justice is the fair treatment and meaningful involvement of all people regardless of race, color, national origin, or income, with respect to the development, implementation, and enforcement of environmental laws, regulations, and policies. EJ is an important part of the struggle to improve and maintain a clean and healthful environment, especially for those who have traditionally lived, worked, and played closest to the sources of pollution (EPA, 2017).”

When interviewing friends, family members, or experts about their experiences in nature or opinions on environmental issues, studnets were encouraged to include a question or two concerning environmental justice.

Students conducted their interviews using Audacity on their computers. Then they saved their recordings to be included on their websites.
